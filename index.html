About Me

I am a Computer Science major pursuing my Bachelor's degree at North Dakota State University (NDSU). My academic focus is in data science, where I enjoy working with data to uncover patterns, insights, and meaningful solutions. Among the technologies I use, SQL is my favorite — I appreciate how powerful and structured it is for organizing, querying, and analyzing data efficiently.

I am passionate about continuously strengthening my technical skills while applying them to real-world problems. I enjoy combining analytical thinking with creativity to build solutions that are both logical and impactful.

Death and Breads
By Medora Sailer (2021)

Death and Breads is a 2021 digital illustration created in a refined pixel‑art style that emphasizes clean silhouettes and expressive character design. The central figure—a stylized reaper rendered in stark black and white—stands out against a patterned backdrop of soft, cartoon bread icons. This contrast between a traditionally ominous character and a whimsical environment creates a playful tension that defines the piece. The work highlights a focus on visual storytelling, deliberate stylistic choices, and the creative potential of limited palettes within retro‑inspired digital art.

Debrah’s Twilight
By Medora Sailer (2020)

Debrah’s Twilight is one of three distinct interpretations created from the same YouTube art tutorial, each produced by a different artist. This version highlights my personal approach to atmosphere and mood, using silhouetted treelines and a soft twilight gradient to emphasize calmness and introspection. The piece demonstrates how artists working from identical instructions can arrive at uniquely expressive results, showcasing the individuality and creative decision‑making behind every artwork.
